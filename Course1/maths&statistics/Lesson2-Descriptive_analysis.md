MEAN(Average)

Definition

The mean is the sum of all values divided by the number of values.
You get the mean by adding up all the numbers and then dividing it by the totality of the numbers
example:

Your five friends have these candies:
Friend 1: 5
Friend 2: 10
Friend 3: 8
Friend 4: 12
Friend 5: 15
Add them: 5 + 10 + 8 + 12 + 15 = 50

There are 5 friends, so divide: 50 Ã· 5 = 10

Mean = 10 candies each (on average).This means that if we decide to share the candies equally without cheating anyone, then Everyone gets a fair share if you share all candies equally!.

Good things about Mean:
It uses every single number. Great when data is nice and even.

MEDIAN(Middle number):

The median is the middle number when data is arranged in order. i.e is the middle number when you arrange numbers from the smallest to the biggest.

Examples:

Example (Odd number of values)

2, 4, 6, 8, 10
Median = 6

Example (Even number of values)

2, 4, 6, 8
Median = 4 + 6 = 10
10/2 = 5

so median = 5

Why Median is cool:
It doesn't care about super big or small numbers. It's like the "real middle" of the group.
Great for things like house prices or salaries â€” where one super rich person doesn't trick you.

This is the most important warning!
Mean can trick you if there are extreme numbers.
Famous Candy Story Gone Wrong:
Four friends have: 5, 5, 5, 5 candies.
Mean = 5. Perfect! Everyone happy.
Now a super rich kid joins with 100 candies.
Numbers: 5, 5, 5, 5, 100
Add up: 120 Ã· 5 = 24 mean.
"Wow! On average, everyone has 24 candies!"
But really? Four kids still have only 5, one has 100. Most kids feel poor!
Median? Line up: 5,5,5,5,100 â†’ Middle is 5. Tells the true story!
Mode = 5.
See? Mean "lied" because of one big number.
This happens in real life:

Average salary in a company: Boss earns millions, workers earn little â†’ Average looks high, but most people earn low.
"Average house price" in a city with some mansions.

Always check all three: Mean, Median, Mode â€” they tell different parts of the story!
Sometimes median is better for "typical" person.
